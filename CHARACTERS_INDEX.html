1
Marcus "Kane" Cole
Protagonist
Character Reference Sheet · 16:9 · 6 Views
Background
15-year-old from the South Bronx, relocated to South Central LA. His mother worked for Victor Cross — barely home. Marcus grew up alone, watching a criminal empire from inside his own apartment.

At age 10, Cross looked down at him and said: "One day you gonna work for me." That was the moment Kane was born — not in the street. In his own living room.
Role in the Story
The protagonist. Rises from a corner kid to controlling the streets — then to leading inside prison. Cold, calculated, zero warmth on the surface. One red line he never crosses: elderly people. They remind him of his grandfather Elijah — the only person who ever tried to protect him.
"He had a good heart once. I saw it. I'm the only one who still sees it." — Elijah Cole

Marcus "Kane" Cole — Older
Narrator — 30 Years Later
Character Reference Sheet · 16:9 · 6 Views
Background
Marcus Cole at 45-50. The same man. 30 years of streets, prison, and survival written on his face and body.

He chose to tell the story himself. Not to justify it. Not to condemn it. Because he is the only one who knows what it actually felt like to be that 15-year-old kid — and he is the only one still alive who remembers who that kid was before the streets finished their work.
Role in the Story
The narrator of the entire film. Speaks in retrospect — he knows the full story because he lived it. The weight in his voice is not performance. The distance in his eyes is not coldness. It is a man looking back at a version of himself he can no longer reach. Kane is still in there. Marcus is trying to explain him.
"I was fifteen. I thought I knew everything. I knew nothing. And I knew everything." — Marcus Cole

Darius Webb
Ride or Die
Character Reference Sheet · 16:9 · 6 Views
Background
15 years old. Grew up in the same building as Marcus — same hallways, same hunger, same pain. A gang beat them both when they were kids. They got off the ground together. No words.

Marcus decided that night: never again. Darius decided: wherever he goes, I go. That was the contract. It was never spoken.
Role in the Story
The soul of the crew. More expressive than Marcus — emotions readable on his face. Not cold like Marcus, warm but hardened. He is not the boss — he is the one who makes the boss human. His blind loyalty is both the most beautiful and most dangerous thing about him.
"From that night on — those two boys were one thing." — Elijah Cole

Victor Cross
Main Antagonist
Character Reference Sheet · 16:9 · 6 Views
Background
Late 40s to early 50s. Established LA street boss. Survived the streets for 25 years through intelligence, ruthlessness, and knowing when to move and when to wait.

Employed Marcus's mother. Looked down at 10-year-old Marcus and said: "One day you gonna work for me." He thought he was recruiting a soldier. He triggered the man who would destroy him.
Role in the Story
The man who started everything — without knowing it. Arrogant, not stupid. Commands genuine fear and respect. His fatal flaw: he still sees Marcus as the little boy from apartment 4B. That blindness destroys him. When they finally face each other — Victor knows. Marcus knows he knows. No pretense.
"I remember when you were this tall." — Victor Cross

Alex Ramos
LAPD Undercover — Law Antagonist
Character Reference Sheet · 16:9 · 6 Views
Background
26 years old. Hispanic - light-skinned, passes for white in most rooms. Grew up on the wrong side. Did things before the badge he doesn't talk about. The department recruited him because he could walk into South Central and disappear. He was good at undercover because he already knew how to be someone else.

Not corrupt for money. Corrupt because the line was always blurry for him. He crossed it so many times undercover he stopped knowing where it was. Genuinely believes he is the good guy. That is the most dangerous thing about him.
Role in the Story
The mirror of Marcus. Both grew up in the same world. Both chose a side. The difference: Marcus is honest about what he is. Alex chose the badge but never left the street behind - the tattoos on his neck prove it. He goes undercover so easily because part of him never left. That part scares him more than Marcus ever could.
"The audience isn't sure who to root for. That's exactly right." — Character Notes

Marcus's Mother
The First Broken Thing He Ever Loved
Character Reference Sheet · 16:9 · 6 Views
Background
Late 20s to early 30s. Worked for Victor Cross — not by choice, by desperation. Rarely home. Marcus was largely alone because of this. She loved Marcus. She just didn't know how to show it in a way that helped him.

The irony: she took the job to give Marcus a better life. She handed him the blueprint for the life she was trying to escape.
Role in the Story
The unconscious lesson. By working for Cross she showed Marcus that this world is real and accessible. She showed him that someone has to be at the bottom. She showed him — without words — that he never wanted to be her. Bad role model not by choice — by desperation.
"A woman asleep on a couch. The apartment is quiet. Marcus is alone." — Scene Notes

7
Simone Hayes
The One Who Waited
Character Reference Sheet · 16:9
Background
Same neighborhood, different path. Smart, grounded, her own life — she did not stumble into Marcus Cole's world by accident. She chose to stay in it with full information.

When Marcus went to Corcoran, she did not disappear. When he walked out the front gate 18 months later, she was there — separate from the crew, on her own terms. She did not rush with them. That is the difference.
Role in the Story
The only person who sees both Marcus and Kane in the same moment — and chooses to stay. Her presence is the show's only warmth. The question the series never answers: was it love, or did she believe a man like that could be saved?
"She was the only person who ever made him feel like being Marcus was enough. He never let himself believe her." — Production Notes
